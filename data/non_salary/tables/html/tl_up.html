ARGENTINA
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
Unemployment insurance premiums, expressed as a share of wages, go into the National Employment Fund. The cost varies across businesses as follows:
  • Large businesses (Mediana Tramo 2) in the services sector (annual sales => ARS 19,012,110,000): 1.08%.
  • Large businesses (Mediana Tramo 2) in the wholesale and retail sectors (annual sales => ARS 66,319,160,000): 1.08%.
  • All other businesses: 0.94%.

CHILE
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
If the worker is under a fixed-term contract, 2.8% of the monthly wage is deposited into an individual severance account (this contribution is paid entirely by the employer). If the worker is under an open-term (indefinite) contract, 2.2% of the monthly wage goes to the individual severance account — 0.6% is paid by the employee and 1.6% by the employer. In addition, the employer pays a contribution to the Solidarity Unemployment Insurance Fund: 0.2% for fixed-term contracts and 0.8% for open-term contracts. Combined, total contributions equal 3.0% of wages. As of 2025, the contribution base is capped at 131.8 Unidades de Fomento (UF).

The Solidarity Fund is designed to help workers whose severance-account balances are insufficient to cover unemployment for a defined period — up to five months for workers on fixed-term contracts and up to three months for workers on open-term contracts.

MEXICO
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
Modality: Authorization to use retirement savings during unemployment spans of more than 45 days.

Protection granted: Workers who entered the contributory pension system at least three years before the unemployment period and have contributed for at least 12 two-month periods since are entitled to receive the equivalent of 30 daily wages with a cap at 10 Federal MW.

Workers who entered the contributory pension system at least five years before the unemployment period are entitled to claim the minimum amount between 90 daily wages (averaged over the last 250 weeks) or 11.5% of their pension savings.

PERU
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
For workers in micro businesses: None.

For workers in small businesses: Half a monthly wage goes into a severance savings account.

For workers in medium and large businesses: One monthly wage goes to a severance savings account.

UNITED STATES
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
California:
Federal level: 0.6-6% (firms may deduct up to 5.4% of UI tax payments made at state level).

Maximum annual contribution base for federal component: USD 7,000.

State level: 3.4% for businesses with less than 3 years of operations, 1.5 - 6.2% for experience-rated businesses depending on termination history and the State's UI Fund Health.

Maximum annual contribution base: USD 7,000.
New York:
Federal level: 0.6-6% (firms may deduct up to 5.4% of UI tax payments made at state level).

Maximum annual contribution base for federal component: USD 7,000.


State level: 4.1% for new businesses, 2.1 - 9.9% after the first 5 quarters depending on employer's history of terminating employees and the overall health of California's UI fund.

Maximum annual contribution base: USD 12,300 set to increase to USD 13,000 by 2026. After 2026, the base will be 16% of the state's average annual wage rounded up to the nearest USD 100.

Florida:
Federal level: 0.6-6% (firms may deduct up to 5.4% of UI tax payments made at state level).

Maximum annual contribution base for federal component: USD 7,000.


State level: 2.7% for new businesses, 0.06 - 8.1% for experience-rated businesses.

Maximum annual contribution base: USD 9,500.

Texas:
Federal level: 0.6-6% (firms may deduct up to 5.4% of UI tax payments made at state level).

Maximum annual contribution base for federal component: USD 7,000.


State level: 2.7% for new businesses, 0 - 6% for experience-rated businesses.

Maximum annual contribution base: USD 9,000.
