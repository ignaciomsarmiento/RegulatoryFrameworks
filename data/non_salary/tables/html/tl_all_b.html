ARGENTINA
Annual and other periodic bonuses
Annual bonus: 1 monthly wage.
Paid leave
Varying by length of tenure:
  • Under 5 years: 14 calendar days.
  • At least 5 years and under 10: 21 calendar days.
  • At least 10 years and under 20: 28 calendar days.
  • At least 20 years: 35 calendar days.
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
Unemployment insurance premiums, expressed as a share of wages, go into the National Employment Fund. The cost varies across businesses as follows:
  • Large businesses (Mediana Tramo 2) in the services sector (annual sales => ARS 19,012,110,000): 1.08%.
  • Large businesses (Mediana Tramo 2) in the wholesale and retail sectors (annual sales => ARS 66,319,160,000): 1.08%.
  • All other businesses: 0.94%.
Other non-wage benefits
Mandatory life insurance: insurance premium of 0.205 per thousand of 5.5 MW. In June 2025, this premium was equal to ARS 334.68 per employee, or 0.001 MW.
Legislation
Law 20744 (1973) - Paid leave.
Decree 567 (1974) - Mandatory life insurance.
Law 23041 (1983) - Annual bonus.
BOLIVIA
Annual and other periodic bonuses
Christmas bonus: 1 monthly wage.

Employment tenure bonus "Antigüedad": 3 MW multiplied by a factor that varies with length of tenure:
  • 2-4 years: 5%.
  • 5-7 years: 11%.
  • 8-10 years: 18%.
  • 11-14 years: 26%.
  • 15-19 years: 34%.
  • 20-24 years: 42%.
  • 25 or more years: 50%.
Quinquennial bonus: Employees who have been in service for at least 5 years are entitled to five monthly wages paid every five years.

Economic growth bonus "Doble aguinaldo": 1 monthly wage awarded when the annual June-to-June GDP growth exceeds 4.5%.

Only employees working within 50km of international borders: Border subsidy: 20% of wages.
Paid leave
Varying by length of tenure:
  • At least 1 year: 15 working days.
  • At least 5 years: 20 working days.
  • At least 10 years: 30 working days.
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
No mandatory unemployment protection.
Other non-wage benefits
Profit sharing (yearly bonus): One annual monthly wage for employees in businesses with positive annual profits or a reduced amount to ensure the bonus cost doesn't exceed 25% of the firm's net profits.

Family allowances: Female employees will receive 4 family subsidies for insured mothers or beneficiaries: a prenatal subsidy equal to BOB 2,000 (0.73 MMW) for each of the last five months of pregnancy, paid in cash or in kind; a natality subsidy of BOB 2,000 as a one-time cash payment per child at birth; a lactation subsidy of BOB 2,000 provided in kind (mainly dairy or equivalent products) during the child’s first 12 months; and a funeral subsidy of BOB 2,000, a one-time cash payment upon the death of a dependent child under 19.
Legislation
General Labor Law (1942, last reformed in 2017) - Paid leave.
Supreme Decree 224 (1943) - Profit sharing and family allowances.
Law of December 18th (1944) - Christmas bonus.
Supreme Decree 21137 (1985) - Bono antiguedad and border subsidy.
Supreme Decree 522 (2010) - Bono quinquenio.
Supreme Decree 1802 (2013) - Doble aguinaldo.
BRAZIL
Annual and other periodic bonuses
Annual bonus: 1 wage.

Vacation bonus: equivalent to 1/3 wages.
Paid leave
Varying by annual unjustified leave days:
  • Less than 5 absences: 30 calendar days.
  • 6 to 14 absences: 24 calendar days.
  • 15 to 23 absences: 18 calendar days.
  • 24 to 32 absences: 12 calendar days.
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
8% contribution over wage income deposited into an individual severance savings account.
Other non-wage benefits
Transport subsidy: Employers must cover displacement costs for employees. They can deduct up to 6% of the employee's salary for this purpose. Calculations are done on a case-by-base basis depending on employee home location and displacement routes, and must be provided through in-kind vouchers.

CHILE
Annual and other periodic bonuses
No mandatory bonuses.
Paid leave
At least 1 year: 15 working days.
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
If the worker is under a fixed-term contract, 2.8% of the monthly wage is deposited into an individual severance account (this contribution is paid entirely by the employer). If the worker is under an open-term (indefinite) contract, 2.2% of the monthly wage goes to the individual severance account — 0.6% is paid by the employee and 1.6% by the employer. In addition, the employer pays a contribution to the Solidarity Unemployment Insurance Fund: 0.2% for fixed-term contracts and 0.8% for open-term contracts. Combined, total contributions equal 3.0% of wages. As of 2025, the contribution base is capped at 131.8 Unidades de Fomento (UF).

The Solidarity Fund is designed to help workers whose severance-account balances are insufficient to cover unemployment for a defined period — up to five months for workers on fixed-term contracts and up to three months for workers on open-term contracts.
Other non-wage benefits
Profit Sharing: Employers must distribute 30% of the company's net taxable profits to employees annually. The distribution is proportional to each employee's remuneration. Alternatively, employers can pay a guaranteed annual bonus equivalent to 25% of each employee's annual salary. Profit sharing is capped at 4.75 MW.
Legislation
Law 18620 (1987) - Paid leave and profit sharing.
Law 20829 (2015) - Unemployment insurance.
COLOMBIA
Annual and other periodic bonuses
Workers with wages under 13MW: 1 wage.
Paid leave
15 working days.
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
Workers with earnings below 13MW: 1/12 monthly wage per month paid to individual severance savings account on January each year + 12% annual interest accrued on monthly dues, paid directly to worker.
Other non-wage benefits
Transport subsidy for workers earning up to 2MW: COP 200,000 in 2025.

Family subsidy: Monthly monetary transfer from Caja de Compensacion Familiar per dependent (18 or younger, 60+ or disabled) to workers earning 4 MW or less.

DOMINICAN REPUBLIC
Annual and other periodic bonuses
Christmas bonus:
  • Workers earning up to 5 MW: 1 wage.
  • Workers earning more than 5 MW: 5 MW.
Paid leave
Varying by length of tenure:
  • At least 1 year and under 5: 14 working days.
  • At least 5 years: 18 working days.
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
No mandatory unemployment protection.
Other non-wage benefits
Profit sharing: Businesses must distribute 10% of their annual profits among employees. Limits on individual profit shares depend on the length of service: workers in service for less than three years may only receive up to 45 days' worth of their regular salary, while workers in service for three years or more are entitled to 70 days' worth of their regular salary, at most. Employees contribute 0.5% of their profit shares to the National Institute providing technical and technological training to workers (INFOTEP).
Legislation
Law 16 (1992) - Annual bonus and profit sharing.
Law 97 (1997) - Paid leave.
ECUADOR
Annual and other periodic bonuses
Christmas bonus: 1 wage.

Annual bonus "Decimocuarta remuneracion": 1 wage.
Paid leave
At least 1 year: 15 calendar days.

After 5 years, workers have the right to one additional vacation day per additional year worked, up to a maximum of 15 extra days, or the equivalent monetary compensation.

Workers younger than 16: 20 calendar days. Workers between 16 and 18: 18 calendar days.
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
3% of wages to individual severance savings account. 2% paid by employee and 1% by employer. Independent workers who make voluntary contributions to SS also make this 3% contribution.
Other non-wage benefits
Profit sharing: Businesses must distribute 15% of their annual profits among employees.

Individual Savings Accounts "Fondo de reserva": For employees with more than one year of service, employers pay one annual monthly wage into an individual savings account administered by the Social Security Administration.

Relocation expenses: Employers must pay for the relocation expenses of new hires and separated workers.
Legislation
Labor Code (2012) - Annual bonuses, paid leave, profit sharing, individual savings accounts, and relocation expenses.
Resolution No. CD 501 of the Board of Directors of the Instituto Ecuatoriano de Seguridad Social (2015) - Unemployment insurance.

MEXICO
Annual and other periodic bonuses
Annual bonus "Aguinaldo": 0.5 wages.

Vacation bonus: bonus equivalent to 25% of the wages corresponding to their vacation period.
Paid leave
Varying by length of tenure:
  • 1 year: 12 working days.
  • After the first year, vacations increase by 2 days per year. Starting in the fifth year, vacations increase by 2 days per every 5 years until they reach a maximum of 32 days.
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
Modality: Authorization to use retirement savings during unemployment spans of more than 45 days.

Protection granted: Workers who entered the contributory pension system at least three years before the unemployment period and have contributed for at least 12 two-month periods since are entitled to receive the equivalent of 30 daily wages with a cap at 10 Federal MW.

Workers who entered the contributory pension system at least five years before the unemployment period are entitled to claim the minimum amount between 90 daily wages (averaged over the last 250 weeks) or 11.5% of their pension savings.
Other non-wage benefits
Profit sharing: Businesses must distribute 10% of their annual profits among their employees. The first 5% is distributed evenly among all eligible employees based on the number of days worked. The second 5% is distributed to employees in proportion to their annual wages. As of 2025, the total employee's payment cannot exceed the highest between three salaries and the average payment of the last three years.
Legislation
Federal Labor Law (1970, last reformed in 2023) - Annual bonuses, paid leave, and profit sharing.
Social Security Law (1995, last reformed in 2023) - Unemployment insurance.

PERU
Annual and other periodic bonuses
Annual bonus: Workers in medium and large businesses: 2 wages + corresponding employer health contribution (9% or 6.75%, depending on modality), annual.

Family bonus: a monthly bonus equivalent to 10% of the MW for workers who have children under 18 or children under 24 attending tertiary education. This bonus is per worker, not per child.

Workers in small and micro businesses: 1 wage + corresponding employer health contribution (9% or 6.75%, depending on modality), annual.
Paid leave
Workers in medium and large businesses: 30 calendar days. Workers may reduce vacation days to 15 calendar days and receive the corresponding monetary compensation for the remaining 15 days.

Workers in micro and small firms: 15 calendar days.
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
For workers in micro businesses: None.

For workers in small businesses: Half a monthly wage goes into a severance savings account.

For workers in medium and large businesses: One monthly wage goes to a severance savings account.
Other non-wage benefits
Profit sharing: Businesses with more than 20 employees must share their annual pre-tax profits with their employees as follows:
  • Businesses in mining, wholesale and retail businesses, and restaurants: 8%
  • Businesses in manufacturing, fishery and telecommunication: 10%
  • All other businesses: 5%.
The first half is distributed among employees based on the number of days worked. The second half is distributed to employees in proportion to their annual wages.

Life insurance: All employers must pay a life insurance premium equivalent to 0.53% of a monthly wage for white-collar employees, 0.71% for blue-collar employees, and 1.46% for those performing high-risk activities. These numbers are estimations published in the Legislative Decree, but the real values that risk insurers charge may vary. Workers' dependents receive 16 monthly wages in case of natural death, or 32 monthly wages in case of accidental death or permanent disability.

UNITED STATES
Annual and other periodic bonuses
No mandatory bonuses at the Federal or State level.
Paid leave
No mandatory annual leave at the Federal or State level.
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
California:
Federal level: 0.6-6% (firms may deduct up to 5.4% of UI tax payments made at state level).

Maximum annual contribution base for federal component: USD 7,000.

State level: 3.4% for businesses with less than 3 years of operations, 1.5 - 6.2% for experience-rated businesses depending on termination history and the State's UI Fund Health.

Maximum annual contribution base: USD 7,000.
New York:
Federal level: 0.6-6% (firms may deduct up to 5.4% of UI tax payments made at state level).

Maximum annual contribution base for federal component: USD 7,000.


State level: 4.1% for new businesses, 2.1 - 9.9% after the first 5 quarters depending on employer's history of terminating employees and the overall health of California's UI fund.

Maximum annual contribution base: USD 12,300 set to increase to USD 13,000 by 2026. After 2026, the base will be 16% of the state's average annual wage rounded up to the nearest USD 100.

Florida:
Federal level: 0.6-6% (firms may deduct up to 5.4% of UI tax payments made at state level).

Maximum annual contribution base for federal component: USD 7,000.


State level: 2.7% for new businesses, 0.06 - 8.1% for experience-rated businesses.

Maximum annual contribution base: USD 9,500.

Texas:
Federal level: 0.6-6% (firms may deduct up to 5.4% of UI tax payments made at state level).

Maximum annual contribution base for federal component: USD 7,000.


State level: 2.7% for new businesses, 0 - 6% for experience-rated businesses.

Maximum annual contribution base: USD 9,000.

Other non-wage benefits
No mandatory other non-wage benefits at the Federal or State level.

SPAIN
Annual and other periodic bonuses
Two extra bonuses are usually paid in June and December, with the option for distribution throughout the year as specified in the contract. Each bonus equals one month's salary.
Paid leave
30 calendar days including public holidays.
Unemployment protection (cost expressed as % of wages)
For open-term contracts: employers pay 5.50% and employees 1.55% for unemployment insurance.

For fixed-term contracts: employers pay 6.70% and employees 1.6% for unemployment insurance.
Other non-wage benefits
No mandatory other non-wage benefits.
Legislation
Workers’ Statute (2015, last reformed in 2024) - Annual bonus, paid leave, and unemployment insurance.
General Social Security Law (1966) - Unemployment insurance.
