Contribution base (CB)
Salaried employees:

Wage contribution base, defined as the monthly average of all wages and benefits. Known fixed annual benefits are proportionally allocated. Unknown variable components of labor income are allocated as per the average of the previous two months.

Employers must calculate contributions based on the employee’s Integrated Daily Wage (Salario Diario Integrado), which adjusts the base salary by a statutory integration factor to account for legally mandated benefits such as vacation days, vacation premium, and Christmas bonus. This factor typically ranges from 1.0493 to 1.036 under minimum legal benefits, increasing with years of service. Employers offering more generous benefits may have higher factors, since the rate depends on the number of vacation days, the vacation premium percentage, and the amount of annual bonus granted.

Minimum contribution base: 1 MW (monthly equivalent).
Maximum contribution base: 25 general MW for the Federal District.
